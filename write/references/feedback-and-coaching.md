# Feedback and Coaching

Use this reference when the user asks to critique prose, learn a reusable writing habit, or receive coaching. Do not rewrite the whole artifact unless the user asks for transformation. Keep feedback specific enough to apply on the next draft.

In feedback-only mode, quote short excerpts and use at most one or two model rewrites for the highest-leverage problems.

## Feedback shape

1. **Keep:** name one or two specific strengths worth preserving.
2. **Change:** name the highest-leverage pattern first; anchor each point to an excerpt, structural choice, or repeated behavior.
3. **Practice:** give one small habit, one short drill, and one before-send check.

Prioritize reader task, structure, evidence and certainty, then sentence-level friction. Do not give generic praise, a long correction list, or advice that could apply unchanged to any draft.

## Useful drills

- **BLUF:** rewrite the first two sentences so the point, ask, or decision appears by sentence two.
- **Paraphrase:** state the message and next action in one sentence without looking; revise the opening if that fails.
- **Specificity:** replace three vague words with a concrete noun, date, number, condition, or evidence label.
- **Voice:** mark three phrases that sound like the writer; preserve them unless they block clarity.
- **Evidence:** label observations, hypotheses, decisions, risks, and recommendations; weaken any claim that outruns its support.
- **Paragraph skim:** read only paragraph openings; revise them if they do not form a useful summary.

When coaching a professional artifact, ask whether the reader can identify purpose, required response, owner, and date. When coaching a material-stakes artifact, recommend the representative-reader test in `rewrite-and-safety.md`.

## Tone

Be direct, calm, and respectful. Explain the reusable pattern, not just the preferred sentence. Preserve the writer's goals and voice; do not treat a stylistic preference as an error without a reader-facing reason.
