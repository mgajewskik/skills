## Why Diátaxis claims there are exactly four kinds

Diátaxis is grounded in two dimensions of craft:
- **action / cognition**
- **acquisition / application**

Those two dimensions fully define the space the framework is concerned with, which is why Diátaxis argues there are **four and only four** documentation forms.

This is meant as a theory of user need, not just a convenient list.

## Quality: what Diátaxis can and cannot do

The framework distinguishes between:

### Functional quality
- accuracy
- completeness
- consistency
- precision
- usefulness

These are real quality requirements, but Diátaxis does **not** create them by itself.

### Deep quality
- fit to human needs
- flow
- anticipation of the user
- feeling good to use
- beauty of overall form

Diátaxis directly helps with this second class of quality by aligning documentation form to reader need.

Important limit:
- Diátaxis can expose functional quality gaps
- Diátaxis cannot replace product knowledge, correctness checks, or editorial verification

## Why the framework matters in practice

The framework is useful because it helps:
- clarify what a page is trying to do
- expose where mixed modes are making the page worse
- improve flow by removing the wrong kind of interruption
- give writers a way to make small, confident improvements without redesigning everything first

## Cautions to preserve

- Do not cite the older Divio presentation as the canonical source when the current site is available.
- Do not mistake the map diagram for a rigid content tree.
- Do not claim Diátaxis alone guarantees accuracy or completeness.
- Do not treat its theory as a replacement for editorial judgment; it is a framework for making better documentation decisions.
