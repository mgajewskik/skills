# Rubric and Checklists

## Pass/Fail rewrite checklist

Pass only if all are true:

- The main point appears in the first 1-2 sentences.
- The reader can tell who does what, by when, and why it matters.
- The structure is easy to scan.
- A reader could paraphrase the main message and next action after one read.
- Most sentences are 20 words or fewer, or longer ones are clearly justified.
- Active voice predominates unless passive is intentional.
- Undefined acronyms, vague pronouns, and `and/or` are removed or made explicit.
- Filler, jargon, and nominalizations are removed unless needed for precision.
- The user's meaning and voice are preserved.
- The revision is clearer, not merely shorter.

## Pass/Fail feedback checklist

Pass only if all are true:

- Feedback points to concrete text patterns or excerpts.
- Feedback starts with the highest-leverage issue.
- Feedback includes at least one strength to keep.
- Feedback includes one repeatable practice habit.
- Feedback gives the user a quick self-check they can run on the next draft.
- Feedback is concise enough to use immediately.

## 8-criteria scoring rubric

Score each category from 1 to 5.
Critical categories: clarity, structure, voice preservation, and factual fidelity.
Target: no critical category below 4.
Target for strong outputs: all categories at 4 or better.

1. Clarity
- 1: hard to understand without rereading
- 3: mostly understandable but muddy in places
- 5: meaning is immediately clear

2. Concision
- 1: padded and repetitive
- 3: some waste remains
- 5: tight without losing substance

3. Structure
- 1: ideas appear in the wrong order
- 3: workable but uneven
- 5: main point first, clean progression

4. Actionability
- 1: no clear takeaway or action
- 3: action exists but is incomplete
- 5: action, owner, timing, and outcome are clear

5. Tone fit
- 1: tone clashes with audience or medium
- 3: acceptable but off in places
- 5: tone fits reader, context, and stakes

6. Voice preservation
- 1: sounds nothing like the user
- 3: some voice remains
- 5: still sounds like the user, only clearer

7. Factual fidelity
- 1: facts or stance drifted
- 3: mostly faithful with some risk
- 5: fully faithful to source meaning and constraints

8. Learnability
- 1: user cannot tell what to repeat next time
- 3: some useful advice
- 5: changes and lessons are reusable

## Anti-criteria

Fail if any are true:
- The rewrite adds facts the user did not provide.
- The rewrite swaps so much wording that the user's voice disappears.
- The rewrite chases readability scores and becomes choppy or incoherent.
- The rewrite leaves the reader guessing about the actor, action, or decision.
- The feedback is generic enough to apply to any text.
